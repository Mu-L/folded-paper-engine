Speakers, looping, autoplay, and background music

Speakers on objects

  • Assign a SpeakerFile path to any object to create an AudioStreamPlayer3D on load.
  • Optional flags: SpeakerLoop, SpeakerVolume (in dB), SpeakerMaxDistance, and SpeakerAutoplay.
  • Looping prefers the audio resource’s native loop support (OGGVorbis/Sample) and falls back to restarting the player when finished.
  • Speakers clean up when their node exits the tree, removing entries from the global speaker map.

Background music

  • Scene-level BackgroundMusic entries create AudioStreamPlayer instances stored on the globals object.
  • BackgroundMusicVolume sets volume before playback. Music is queued and started via the audio utils after scene load.
  • Music stops and globals clear during level unload so new scenes can repopulate the playlist.

Triggering audio

  • Use the SpeakerTrigger and SpeakerTriggerSelf commands from triggers or frame events to play audio programmatically.
  • Pair triggers with proximity sensors to start or fade sounds when the player enters an area.
