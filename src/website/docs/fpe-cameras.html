Cameras
Player camera defaults, cutscenes, and switching

Player camera setup

  • When Player Controls are enabled, FPE spawns a following camera, registers it globally, and makes it current.
  • Use Standard Camera Height and Standard Camera Distance in the Player Controls panel to tune third-person framing. First-person locks to the head and clamps pitch to ±90°.
  • If you enable Can Hold Items, the camera also keeps a Hold Zone in front of the view so pickups appear where the player is looking.

Switching cameras with commands

  1. Create any additional cameras you need in Godot (cutscene cams, security cams, overhead cams) and give them clear node names.
  2. In Blender, add a trigger or animation event that runs the Activate Camera command and point it at the camera node you want to use.
  3. When the moment ends, switch back however you prefer: run Reactivate Player Camera if you previously deactivated it, or simply activate another camera to take over.

Cutscenes and UI

  • Pair camera switches with Deactivate Player Controls to keep gameplay paused during cutscenes, and Reactivate Player Controls when you’re done.
  • For menu screens that need a static camera, activate that camera before showing UI, then restore gameplay controls and the player camera after the menu closes.
  • If a camera stays active unexpectedly, look for a missing command or animation event that should have switched back.
