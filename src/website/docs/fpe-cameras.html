Cameras
Player camera settings, swaps, and best practices

Player camera basics

  1. Select your Player object in Blender and open Player Controls.
  2. Choose the control style (First Person, Third Person, or Top Down) and adjust look sensitivity to match your project.
  3. If you want the player to hold items, enable Can Hold Items so the runtime attaches holdable items to the camera view.

Managing multiple cameras

  • Place additional cameras in the scene for cutscenes or fixed angles. Name them clearly (for example, “DoorCam” or “BossIntro”).
  • Use Event Commands or triggers to activate a camera when needed, then switch back to the player camera when the sequence ends.
  • You only need to call Reactivate Player Camera if you previously deactivated it for a cutscene.

Quick camera tips

  • Keep UI Elements within the chosen camera’s view so crosshairs and overlays stay visible.
  • Apply transforms in Blender before export to avoid offset cameras in Godot.
  • Test sensitivity and field of view early; small adjustments can make the controls feel polished for Creator Users.
